Section authoring guidance

Keep section markup predictable so teams can compose whole pages from stable blocks instead of drifting into one-off layouts. The default foundation is one full-width section with one inner container.

Foundation first

Start with <section class="section"><div class="container">...</div></section>. Add feature-section, service-section or other ready wrappers inside that band when the content needs a more designed shell.

What belongs on this page

  • hero, proof, service and pricing compositions
  • FAQ, testimonial and CTA wrappers
  • footer patterns and repeated closing-page structures

What stays elsewhere

  • Use components.html for primitives and interaction shells such as buttons, dropdowns, tabs, modal and scrollspy.
  • Use forms.html for form controls, validation states and the reusable contact section.
  • Use layout.html and utilities.html when the page needs structural or spacing helpers.

Composition order

  1. Open with hero or compact intro.
  2. Add feature, service or proof content.
  3. Support decisions with pricing, FAQ or testimonials.
  4. Close with one decisive CTA and a low-noise footer.

Semantic rules

  • Keep headings as real heading elements and preserve their hierarchy.
  • Keep pricing features, proof points and footer links as real lists.
  • Reuse section wrappers before inventing project-specific shells.
