# FNLLA Web Code of Conduct

## Our standard

FNLLA Web is maintained as a professional working environment by TechAyo LTD (techayo.co.uk).

Everyone participating in this repository, its issue tracker, pull requests, release discussions and related collaboration spaces is expected to act with respect, professionalism and care for other people.

We want FNLLA Web to remain a place where maintainers, collaborators, contractors, clients and invited contributors can work productively without harassment, hostility or needless friction.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that support this standard include:

- communicating clearly, respectfully and in good faith
- giving constructive technical feedback without personal attacks
- focusing reviews on code, documentation, behavior and risk rather than on individuals
- respecting differing experience levels, backgrounds and viewpoints
- taking responsibility for mistakes and helping resolve them
- keeping commercial, client and repository context confidential where required

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, intimidation, threats or sustained hostility
- insulting, demeaning or discriminatory language
- personal attacks in public or private channels
- publishing private, client, business or security-sensitive information without authorization
- sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention
- disruptive trolling, bad-faith argument or repeated escalation after a moderation decision
- misuse of the repository to pressure maintainers into accepting off-scope work

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in:

- GitHub issues, pull requests and reviews
- commit messages and proposed patches
- release and maintenance discussions
- direct collaboration carried out as part of FNLLA Web work
- other spaces where someone is representing FNLLA Web or TechAyo LTD in relation to this repository

## Maintainer responsibilities

TechAyo LTD and repository maintainers may:

- moderate or remove comments, issues, pull requests or other content
- pause or close discussions that are no longer constructive
- reject participation that conflicts with this Code of Conduct, repository scope or business obligations
- restrict or remove access where necessary to protect people, clients, operations or the integrity of the project

Moderation decisions will aim to be proportionate, practical and fair, while recognizing that this is a maintainer-led business repository rather than a general community governance commons.

## Reporting

If you need to report unacceptable behavior, use TechAyo LTD's contact route at `https://techayo.co.uk` and clearly label the message `FNLLA Web Code of Conduct`.

Include:

- what happened
- where it happened
- links or screenshots if available
- whether there is any immediate safety, privacy or client-risk concern

Reports will be reviewed as promptly and discreetly as practical. Privacy will be respected as far as possible, though maintainers may need to involve business or legal stakeholders where risk, security, client confidentiality or repeated misconduct is involved.

## Enforcement

Depending on severity and context, responses may include:

- a private warning
- removal of comments or contributions
- closure of an issue or pull request
- temporary participation limits
- permanent removal from repository collaboration spaces
